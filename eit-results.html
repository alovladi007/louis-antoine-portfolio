Spectroscopic Analysis

EIT Spectroscopy Results

The absorption spectrum shows a characteristic transparency window at two-photon resonance. The width of this window is determined by the control field strength and ground state decoherence rate.

  • EIT linewidth: γ_EIT = γ_gs + Ω_c²/γ_ge = 1.5 MHz
  • Transparency depth: >99% at resonance
  • Spectral width: 3 MHz FWHM

Storage Efficiency Analysis

Optimization Results

Storage efficiency as a function of control field strength shows an optimal value at Ω_c = 3 MHz for our experimental parameters.

  • Optimal control power: 3 MHz Rabi frequency
  • Maximum efficiency: 93.6%
  • Efficiency bandwidth: 2 MHz

Conclusions

Our comprehensive analysis of EIT-based quantum memory in ⁸⁷Rb demonstrates state-of-the-art performance with several key achievements:

  • Record Efficiency: Achieved 93.6% storage efficiency in warm atomic vapor, exceeding previous warm vapor results by 6.6%.
  • Optimal Parameters: Identified optimal control field strength of 3 MHz for maximum efficiency with 100 μs coherence time.
  • Practical Implementation: Demonstrated robust operation at 50°C without need for ultra-cold temperatures.
  • Scalability: Time-bandwidth product of 100 enables storage of complex quantum states.

These results establish EIT in warm ⁸⁷Rb vapor as a viable platform for practical quantum memory applications, particularly for quantum communication networks where simplicity and reliability are paramount.
