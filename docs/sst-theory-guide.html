Theory Guide: Mathematical Foundations

Detailed mathematical derivations for SST analysis and design

1. Dual Active Bridge (DAB) Power Flow Equations

Fundamental Power Transfer Equation: \[ P = \frac{n V_1 V_2}{2\pi f_s L_{lk}} \phi \left(1 - \frac{|\phi|}{\pi}\right) \]

Derivation:

Start with the voltage across leakage inductance: \[ v_{Llk}(t) = L_{lk} \frac{di_{Llk}(t)}{dt} \]
For square wave voltages with phase shift φ: \[ v_{Llk}(t) = v_1(t) - n \cdot v_2(t - \phi/\omega_s) \]
Integrate to find inductor current: \[ i_{Llk}(t) = \frac{1}{L_{lk}} \int_0^t [v_1(\tau) - n \cdot v_2(\tau - \phi/\omega_s)] d\tau \]
Average power over one switching period: \[ P = \frac{1}{T_s} \int_0^{T_s} v_1(t) \cdot i_{Llk}(t) dt \]
After integration and simplification: \[ P = \frac{n V_1 V_2}{2\pi f_s L_{lk}} \phi \left(1 - \frac{|\phi|}{\pi}\right) \]
Note: Maximum power transfer occurs at φ = ±π/2, giving: \[ P_{max} = \frac{n V_1 V_2}{4 f_s L_{lk}} \]

2. Zero Voltage Switching (ZVS) Conditions

ZVS Constraint: \[ i_{Llk}(t_{switch}) \cdot \text{sign}(v_{switch}) > 0 \] Minimum Current for ZVS: \[ |i_{ZVS,min}| = \frac{4 C_{oss} V_{ds}}{t_{dead}} \]

ZVS Analysis:

Energy stored in output capacitance: \[ E_{Coss} = \frac{1}{2} C_{oss} V_{ds}^2 \]
This energy must be removed by inductor current: \[ \frac{1}{2} L_{lk} i_{ZVS}^2 > E_{Coss} \]
During dead time, capacitor must discharge: \[ i_{Llk} \cdot t_{dead} = C_{oss} \cdot V_{ds} \]
ZVS region boundary: \[ \phi_{ZVS,min} = \frac{2\pi f_s L_{lk} i_{ZVS,min}}{n V_2} \]
Parameter Symbol Typical Value Unit
Output Capacitance C_oss 100-500 pF
Dead Time t_dead 100-200 ns
Leakage Inductance L_lk 10-50 μH

3. Thermal Network Modeling

Foster Thermal Network: \[ Z_{th}(s) = \sum_{i=1}^{n} \frac{R_{th,i}}{1 + s \tau_i} \] Junction Temperature: \[ T_j(t) = T_a + P_{loss} \cdot Z_{th}(t) \]

Thermal Impedance Calculation:

Thermal capacitance: \[ C_{th,i} = \frac{\tau_i}{R_{th,i}} \]
Time domain response: \[ T_j(t) = T_a + P_{loss} \sum_{i=1}^{n} R_{th,i}(1 - e^{-t/\tau_i}) \]
Steady-state temperature: \[ T_{j,ss} = T_a + P_{loss} \sum_{i=1}^{n} R_{th,i} \]
Temperature-dependent losses: \[ R_{ds,on}(T) = R_{ds,on,25°C} \cdot [1 + \alpha(T - 25)] \]
Critical Limits:
• SiC MOSFET: T_j,max = 175°C (continuous), 200°C (peak)
• GaN HEMT: T_j,max = 150°C (continuous), 175°C (peak)
• Safety margin: Operate at ≤80% of maximum

4. Power Loss Calculations

Total Loss Model: \[ P_{loss,total} = P_{cond} + P_{sw} + P_{mag} + P_{aux} \]

Component Losses:

Conduction Loss: \[ P_{cond} = I_{rms}^2 \cdot R_{ds,on}(T_j) \] \[ I_{rms} = I_{dc} \sqrt{D + \frac{1}{3}\left(\frac{\Delta I}{I_{dc}}\right)^2} \]
Switching Loss: \[ P_{sw} = f_s \cdot (E_{on} + E_{off}) + \frac{1}{2} f_s C_{oss} V_{ds}^2 \] \[ E_{on/off} = \frac{1}{2} V_{ds} I_{d} t_{r/f} \]
Magnetic Core Loss (Steinmetz): \[ P_{core} = k \cdot f^{\alpha} \cdot B_{max}^{\beta} \cdot V_{core} \] Typical: k = 0.05, α = 1.5, β = 2.4
Copper Loss in Transformer: \[ P_{cu} = I_{pri,rms}^2 R_{pri} + I_{sec,rms}^2 R_{sec} \] With skin effect: \( R_{ac} = R_{dc} \cdot F_R \)

5. Advanced Control Strategies

Single Phase Shift (SPS): \[ \phi_1 = \phi_2 = 0, \quad \phi \in [-\pi/2, \pi/2] \] Double Phase Shift (DPS): \[ \phi_1 = -\phi_2 = \phi/2 \] Triple Phase Shift (TPS): \[ \phi_1 \neq 0, \quad \phi_2 \neq 0, \quad \phi = \phi_1 - \phi_2 \]

TPS Optimization:

Objective: Minimize RMS current \[ \min_{D_1,D_2,\phi} \quad I_{rms} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{T_s} \int_0^{T_s} i_{Llk}^2(t) dt} \]
Subject to power constraint: \[ P = P_{ref} \]
ZVS constraints: \[ i_{Llk}(t_{on,k}) > i_{ZVS,min} \quad \forall k \in \{switches\} \]
Benefits of TPS:
• Extended ZVS range (20-100% load vs 40-100% for SPS)
• Reduced RMS current (up to 30% reduction)
• Lower switching losses
• Improved efficiency at light loads
