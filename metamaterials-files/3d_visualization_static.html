Magnetic Response

SRRs create artificial magnetic dipoles through induced circulating currents, enabling magnetic response at optical frequencies where natural magnetism is negligible.

Resonance Behavior

The LC resonance frequency is determined by the ring inductance and gap capacitance, typically designed for operation between 1-10 THz for optical metamaterials.

Plasma Behavior

Wire arrays exhibit an effective plasma frequency below which electromagnetic waves cannot propagate, creating a tunable high-pass filter response.

Polarization Control

The anisotropic nature of wire arrays enables strong polarization-dependent response, useful for polarizers and wave plates at THz and optical frequencies.

Hyperbolic Dispersion

Alternating metal-dielectric layers create hyperbolic dispersion, supporting high-k modes that enable subdiffraction imaging and enhanced spontaneous emission.

Tunable Properties
