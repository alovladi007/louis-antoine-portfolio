Metamaterials Theory Handbook

1. Effective Medium Theory

Metamaterials can be described by effective permittivity and permeability when the unit cell size is much smaller than the wavelength.

n_eff = √(ε_eff × μ_eff)
Z = √(μ_eff / ε_eff)

2. Negative Refraction

When both ε and μ are negative, the refractive index becomes negative, leading to reversed Snell's law.

n₁sin(θ₁) = -n₂sin(θ₂)
Phase velocity: v_p = c/n (opposite to group velocity)

3. Transformation Optics

Coordinate transformations in Maxwell's equations lead to spatially varying material parameters.

ε' = JεJ^T / det(J)
μ' = JμJ^T / det(J)
where J is the Jacobian matrix

4. Lorentz-Drude Models

Frequency-dependent material parameters for metamaterials.

ε(ω) = 1 - ωₚ²/(ω² + iγₑω) [Drude]
μ(ω) = 1 + Fω²/(ω₀² - ω² - iγₘω) [Lorentz]

5. S-Parameter Retrieval

Extracting effective parameters from measured/simulated S-parameters.

Z = ±√[(1+S₁₁)² - S₂₁²]/[(1-S₁₁)² - S₂₁²]
n = (1/k₀d) × arccos[(1-S₁₁²+S₂₁²)/(2S₂₁)]
