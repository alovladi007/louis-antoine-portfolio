GaN Power Device Characterization

Thermal Modeling of InGaN/GaN HEMTs

Project Overview

Comprehensive analysis and optimization of GaN-based High Electron Mobility Transistors (HEMTs) for high-efficiency power applications. This project encompasses thermal modeling, device simulation, and performance characterization using multiple computational approaches.

Key Features

  • 3D thermal modeling and heat dissipation analysis
  • Temperature-dependent material properties
  • Electro-thermal coupling simulations
  • Multi-physics integration including piezoelectric effects
  • Optimization for high-power RF applications

Key Results

Thermal Performance

  • Junction temperature: 127°C at 2W power dissipation
  • Thermal resistance: 50 K/W with SiC substrate
  • 15% temperature reduction with field plate design

Device Structure

  • In0.17Al0.83N barrier layer (15 nm)
  • GaN channel (300 nm)
  • SiC substrate for optimal heat dissipation
