Neuromorphic Computing Research

Partitioned Crossbar Memristor Accelerator for Energy-Efficient ANNs

Project Overview

Investigation of memristor-based neuromorphic computing architectures for AI applications. This research focuses on developing a partitioned crossbar memristor accelerator that leverages the unique properties of memristive devices to achieve unprecedented energy efficiency and computational capabilities for artificial neural networks.

Key Features

  • Partitioned crossbar array design for improved scalability
  • Memristor-based synaptic weight storage with analog computation
  • Energy-efficient matrix-vector multiplication
  • SPICE-based circuit simulations and verification
  • Python/TensorFlow integration for neural network mapping
  • Hardware-software co-design methodology
