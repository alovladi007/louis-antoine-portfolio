Associate of Science in Engineering Science

CT State Community College Housatonic - Bridgeport, CT (2013-2015)

Program Overview

The Engineering Science program provided a comprehensive foundation in mathematics, physics, and chemistry, preparing students for transfer to four-year engineering and science programs. This rigorous curriculum emphasized analytical thinking, problem-solving skills, and hands-on laboratory experience.

Mathematics

Calculus I

Limits, derivatives, and integrals of single-variable functions. Applications to physics and engineering problems including optimization and related rates.

Calculus II

Integration techniques, applications of integration, infinite sequences and series, parametric equations, and polar coordinates.

Multivariable Calculus

Vectors, partial derivatives, multiple integrals, vector calculus including Green's, Stokes', and divergence theorems.

Differential Equations

First and second-order differential equations, systems of linear equations, Laplace transforms, and applications to physical systems.

Physics

Calculus-Based Physics I

Mechanics including kinematics, dynamics, energy, momentum, rotational motion, and oscillations. Laboratory experiments with error analysis.

Calculus-Based Physics II

Electricity and magnetism, electromagnetic waves, optics, and modern physics introduction. Advanced laboratory techniques and data analysis.

Chemistry

General Chemistry I

Atomic structure, chemical bonding, stoichiometry, gas laws, thermochemistry, and introduction to quantum mechanics. Laboratory emphasis on quantitative analysis.

General Chemistry II

Chemical kinetics, equilibrium, acid-base chemistry, electrochemistry, and thermodynamics. Advanced laboratory techniques including spectroscopy.
