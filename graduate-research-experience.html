Graduate Research Assistant / TA

University of Connecticut - Storrs, CT (October 2022 - April 2024)

Department: Electrical & Computer Engineering

Role Overview

Served as Graduate Research Assistant and Teaching Assistant in the Electrical & Computer Engineering department, supporting advanced semiconductor and optoelectronics laboratories while conducting cutting-edge research in quantum optics and semiconductor device physics.

Laboratory Support & Research

Semiconductor & Optoelectronics Labs

Supported semiconductor and optoelectronics labs with precision measurements, front-end and back-end processing technologies including interconnect and dual Damascene processes.

Lithography Expertise

Specialized in optical pattern formation, photoresists, wafer steppers, and scanners. Extensive work with overlay techniques, immersion lithography, and extreme ultraviolet (EUV) lithography systems.

Precision Measurements

Conducted high-precision measurements using advanced characterization equipment for semiconductor device analysis and process optimization.

Advanced Semiconductor Processing

Doping & Ion Implantation

Worked with doping in semiconductors including diffusion processes, design and evaluation of diffused layers, and dopant diffusion in compound semiconductors. Experience with Monte Carlo implant profiles.

Etching Technologies

Expertise in wet etching, dry/plasma etching, and atomic layer etching. Performed etchback processes using photoresist and spin-on glass (SOG), with familiarity in Applied Materials Reflection CMP machine (Mirra CMP 200mm).

Deposition Technologies

Extensive experience with CVD, ELO, LPCVD, MOCVD, PECVD, ALD, and molecular beam epitaxy (MBE) for advanced thin film deposition and material engineering.

RF Engineering & Quantum Optics

RF Circuit Design

Engineered RF circuits and antennas for atom-based sensors operating in the GHz regime, coupled with electromagnetic simulation software (CST, HFSS) for optimization and analysis.

Quantum Sensor Development

Developed advanced atom-based sensors with applications in precision measurements and quantum sensing technologies.

EM Simulation

Utilized advanced electromagnetic simulation tools including CST Studio Suite and HFSS for RF component design and optimization.

Teaching & Mentorship

Student Guidance

Provided guidance to undergraduate students working on independent projects related to semiconductor processing, quantum optics, and RF engineering research.

Laboratory Instruction

Assisted in teaching laboratory courses, demonstrating proper use of advanced characterization equipment and safety protocols in cleanroom environments.

Research Mentorship

Mentored students on research methodologies, data analysis techniques, and scientific writing for technical publications and presentations.

Research Focus Areas

Semiconductor Device Physics

Advanced research in semiconductor device characterization, process optimization, and novel device architectures for next-generation electronics.

RF & Microwave Engineering

Design and optimization of RF circuits, antennas, and microwave components for quantum sensor applications and high-frequency electronics.
